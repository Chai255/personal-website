Serengeti National Park

Home to the Great Migration and one of the world's most spectacular wildlife spectacles

Park Overview

🦁 About Serengeti

The Serengeti National Park is Tanzania's oldest and most famous national park, covering 14,750 square kilometers of vast plains, savannah, and woodlands.

  • Size: 14,750 km² (5,700 sq mi)
  • Established: 1951
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site
  • Best Known For: The Great Migration

🌍 Geography & Ecosystems

The Serengeti features diverse landscapes including endless plains, acacia woodlands, and riverine forests.

  • Serengeti Plains: Vast grasslands perfect for grazing
  • Kopjes: Ancient granite outcrops
  • Grumeti River: Permanent water source
  • Woodlands: Acacia and Terminalia trees

🦓 The Great Migration

Witness one of nature's greatest spectacles as over 1.5 million wildebeest, 200,000 zebra, and 300,000 gazelle migrate in search of fresh grazing.

  • Timing: Year-round, but varies by season
  • River Crossings: Most dramatic in July-October
  • Calving Season: February-March in southern plains
  • Predators: Lions, cheetahs, and hyenas follow the herds

Wildlife & Viewing Areas

🦁 Big Five

The Serengeti is home to all of Africa's Big Five: lions, leopards, elephants, buffalo, and rhinos. Lions are particularly abundant with over 3,000 individuals.

🦁 Lions 🐆 Leopards 🐘 Elephants

🌅 Central Serengeti

Known as "Seronera," this area offers year-round wildlife viewing with resident predators and abundant herbivores. Great for first-time visitors.

🦁 Resident Lions 🐆 Leopards 🌿 Year-round Game

🏞️ Southern Plains

Vast grasslands where the migration herds gather during the wet season (December-May). Spectacular during calving season in February-March.

🦓 Migration Herds 🐣 Calving Season 🌿 Lush Grasslands

🌊 Western Corridor

Home to the Grumeti River where dramatic river crossings occur during the migration. Excellent for crocodile and hippo sightings.

🌊 River Crossings 🐊 Crocodiles 🦛 Hippos

🌲 Northern Serengeti

Less visited area with beautiful landscapes and the Mara River. Excellent for seeing the migration in July-October and avoiding crowds.

🌊 Mara River 👥 Fewer Crowds 🏔️ Beautiful Scenery

🦅 Birdlife

Over 500 bird species including vultures, eagles, ostriches, and colorful bee-eaters. Excellent for birdwatching enthusiasts.

🦅 Birds of Prey 🦩 Colorful Species 📸 Photography

Best Time to Visit

🌧️ Wet Season (November-May)

Lush landscapes, fewer tourists, and the calving season in February-March. Some roads may be impassable during heavy rains.

☀️ Dry Season (June-October)

Best wildlife viewing, dramatic river crossings, and comfortable temperatures. Peak tourist season with higher prices.

🦓 Migration Highlights

February-March: Calving in southern plains. July-October: River crossings in western and northern areas.

📅 Recommended Months

June-October for migration and dry season game viewing. February-March for calving season and fewer crowds.

Safari Tips & Safety

🚗 Game Drive Etiquette

  • Stay in your vehicle at all times (except at designated areas)
  • Keep noise levels low to avoid disturbing wildlife
  • Don't feed or approach animals
  • Follow your guide's instructions carefully

📸 Photography Tips

  • Bring extra batteries and memory cards
  • Use a telephoto lens for wildlife shots
  • Shoot during golden hours (dawn and dusk)
  • Be patient and wait for the perfect moment

Accommodation & Getting There

🏨 Where to Stay

Options range from luxury lodges to tented camps. Popular areas include Seronera (central), Grumeti (western), and northern Serengeti for migration viewing.

✈️ Getting There

Fly to Seronera Airstrip from Arusha, Dar es Salaam, or Zanzibar. Alternatively, drive from Arusha (6-8 hours) or combine with other northern parks.

💰 Costs

Park fees: $70 USD per person per day. Safari packages range from $200-800+ per person per day depending on accommodation level.

🎯 Safari Duration

Minimum 2-3 days recommended. 5-7 days ideal for comprehensive wildlife viewing and migration experience.

Tanzania: Mini-Wiki

Essential information for first-time visitors to Tanzania

Survival Basics

💰 Mobile Money

Tanzania runs on mobile money! M-Pesa is the most popular service. You can pay for almost everything - from street food to transport to shopping.

  • Download M-Pesa app before arrival
  • Register with your passport at any M-Pesa agent
  • Keep small denominations for daily use

🗣️ Essential Swahili Phrases

Learning a few Swahili phrases goes a long way with locals:

  • Jambo - Hello
  • Asante - Thank you
  • Karibu - Welcome
  • Hakuna matata - No worries
  • Pole pole - Slowly, take it easy
  • Mzuri sana - Very good

🚌 Getting Around

Public transport in Tanzania is an adventure in itself:

  • Daladala - Minibuses, very cheap but crowded
  • Boda boda - Motorcycle taxis, negotiate price first
  • Bajaji - Three-wheeled taxis, good for short trips
  • Uber - Available in major cities

🌡️ Weather & Best Time to Visit

Tanzania has two main seasons:

  • Dry Season (June-October): Best for safaris, cooler weather
  • Wet Season (November-May): Lush landscapes, fewer tourists
  • Coastal areas are hot and humid year-round

Places to Visit

Dar es Salaam

The bustling commercial capital and largest city. Known for its vibrant food scene, beautiful beaches, and mix of modern and traditional culture.

🍽️ Food Capital 🏖️ Beaches 🛍️ Markets

Zanzibar

The "Spice Island" with pristine beaches, historic Stone Town, and rich cultural heritage. Perfect for relaxation and cultural immersion.

The safari capital of Tanzania, gateway to the Northern Circuit. Close to Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, and Mount Kilimanjaro.

Practical Information

📋 Visa Requirements

Most nationalities need a visa. Apply online or get on arrival at major airports. Cost: $50-100 USD.

💉 Health & Vaccines

Yellow fever certificate required. Malaria prophylaxis recommended. Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel.

💱 Currency

Tanzanian Shilling (TZS). USD widely accepted. Credit cards work in major hotels and restaurants.

🔌 Electricity

Type G (British) plugs. Voltage: 230V. Power cuts common in rural areas.

Safety & Cultural Tips

🛡️ General Safety

  • Keep valuables secure and avoid displaying expensive items
  • Use registered taxis or ride-sharing apps
  • Be cautious at night, especially in unfamiliar areas
  • Keep copies of important documents

🤝 Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly, especially in rural areas
  • Greet people with "Jambo" and a handshake
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people
  • Remove shoes when entering homes or mosques

Zanzibar

The "Spice Island" - A paradise of pristine beaches, historic Stone Town, and rich cultural heritage

Island Overview

🏝️ About Zanzibar

Zanzibar is a semi-autonomous archipelago off the coast of Tanzania, famous for its spices, pristine beaches, and the historic Stone Town - a UNESCO World Heritage site.

  • Main Island: Unguja (often called Zanzibar)
  • Capital: Stone Town
  • Population: ~1.3 million people
  • Language: Swahili, English, Arabic

🌍 Geography & Climate

Located in the Indian Ocean, Zanzibar has a tropical climate with two distinct seasons.

  • Dry Season: June-October (best time to visit)
  • Rainy Season: March-May (heavy rains, some roads impassable)
  • Temperature: 25-32°C year-round
  • Humidity: 70-85%

✈️ Getting There

Zanzibar is accessible by air and sea from mainland Tanzania.

  • Airport: Abeid Amani Karume International Airport (ZNZ)
  • From Dar: 20-minute flight or 2-hour ferry
  • International: Direct flights from Europe and Middle East
  • Ferry: Daily ferries from Dar es Salaam

Must-See Attractions

🏛️ Stone Town

The historic heart of Zanzibar, featuring narrow alleys, traditional Swahili architecture, and the famous House of Wonders. A UNESCO World Heritage site.

🏛️ Architecture 📜 History 🛍️ Shopping

🌴 Nungwi Beach

One of Zanzibar's most beautiful beaches, known for its white sand, turquoise waters, and excellent diving and snorkeling opportunities.

🏖️ White Sand 🤿 Diving 🌅 Sunsets

🌶️ Spice Farms

Experience Zanzibar's famous spice heritage with guided tours of working spice farms. Learn about cloves, nutmeg, vanilla, and more.

🌶️ Spices 🌿 Nature 🎓 Education

🐢 Prison Island

Small island with giant tortoises, beautiful beaches, and snorkeling spots. Great for a half-day trip from Stone Town.

🐢 Tortoises 🏖️ Beaches 🤿 Snorkeling

🌊 Paje Beach

Famous for kiteboarding and windsurfing, Paje offers wide beaches and consistent winds. Perfect for water sports enthusiasts.

🏄 Kiteboarding 🏖️ Wide Beaches 💨 Wind Sports

🕌 Old Fort

Historic fort built by the Portuguese in the 17th century, now hosting cultural events and a craft market. Great views of the harbor.

🏰 History 🛍️ Crafts 📸 Views

Activities & Experiences

🤿 Water Activities

Diving, snorkeling, fishing, and boat trips. The coral reefs around Zanzibar are teeming with marine life including turtles, dolphins, and colorful fish.

🏄 Adventure Sports

Kiteboarding, windsurfing, and kayaking are popular, especially in Paje and Jambiani. Equipment rental and lessons available.

🚶 Cultural Tours

Walking tours of Stone Town, visits to local villages, and traditional music performances. Learn about Swahili culture and history.

🌅 Sunset Cruises

Traditional dhow boat trips at sunset, often including dinner and traditional music. Perfect for romantic evenings.

Food & Culture

🍽️ Local Cuisine

  • Seafood: Fresh fish, prawns, and octopus are excellent
  • Spices: Try pilau rice, biryani, and spiced tea
  • Street Food: Zanzibar pizza, urojo soup, and fresh fruit
  • Drinks: Fresh coconut water, spiced coffee, and local beer

🤝 Cultural Etiquette

  • Dress modestly, especially in Stone Town and rural areas
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people
  • Remove shoes when entering homes or mosques
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Accommodation & Getting Around

🏨 Where to Stay

Stone Town for culture and history, Nungwi for beaches and nightlife, Paje for water sports, and Kendwa for quiet relaxation.

🚗 Transportation

Rent a car for flexibility, use local taxis for short trips, or book organized tours. Roads can be rough in rural areas.

💰 Costs

Zanzibar can be expensive compared to mainland Tanzania. Budget $50-200+ per day depending on accommodation and activities.

⏰ Best Time to Visit

June-October for dry weather and calm seas. Avoid March-May due to heavy rains and rough seas.
